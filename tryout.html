How PULSE Works

A simple lifecycle: source assets → structure pools → route cashflows → distribute → report. Real estate and financing are the core focus.

1

Asset sourcing (Real estate & financing)

Pools target cashflow-producing RWAs. Examples: real-estate income notes, invoice financing, short-duration credit structures, and diversified RWA baskets.

2

Structuring & pool terms

Each pool defines terms (duration, limits, payout schedule, fees if any, and risk disclosures). Terms are published clearly and updated transparently.

3

Cashflow routing on-chain

When off-chain cashflows occur, the protocol routes distributions on-chain according to pool rules, enabling verifiable allocation and predictable mechanics.

4

Distribution & reporting

Distributions and key events are logged where possible. Reporting is delivered via periodic updates: allocations, performance, and risk notes — so users can follow value flow.

FAQ

What is PULSE?

PULSE is a Solana-native DeFi protocol designed to route real-world asset (RWA) cashflows on-chain. It focuses primarily on real estate and financing pools, with transparent terms and reporting.

What RWAs does PULSE focus on?

The core focus is real estate (income notes, structured cashflows) and financing (short-duration pools such as invoices or credit-like exposures). Over time, the protocol can expand into broader RWA categories.

Where does yield come from?

Yield is intended to come from underlying asset cashflows (repayments, interest, discounts, rental-like income streams), not from “printing tokens.” Each pool must publish its specific cashflow model and distribution rules.

Is yield guaranteed?

No. RWAs and DeFi carry risk: market risk, counterparty/operational risk, legal/regulatory risk, and smart-contract risk. PULSE does not guarantee returns.

What is the role of the PLS token?

PLS is designed for governance (proposals, voting on parameters) and staking (incentives aligned with long-term participation). Any fee distribution or reward model should be explicitly documented before activation.

How does staking work?

Staking is planned to provide a clear participation mechanism. When staking is enabled, PULSE will publish: reward sources, emission schedule (if any), lock/unstake rules, and distribution frequency. You can view the staking UI at staking.html.

How do I evaluate a pool?

Look for published pool terms: duration, exposure limits, payout schedule, fees (if any), and risk disclosures. Transparent reporting should include material events and changes to pool parameters.

Has the protocol been audited?

If/when audits are completed, they should be linked publicly with scope, date, and the audited commit/version. Until then, treat the protocol as pre-launch software and use caution.

Which wallets are supported?

The connect button uses the standard Solana provider flow (e.g., Phantom-compatible). A legitimate dApp never asks for your seed phrase. Always verify the website domain.

Are there fees?

Fees (if any) should be disclosed per pool (management/protocol/performance) and how they are used (treasury, maintenance, incentives). If fees aren’t finalized, they should be explicitly marked as TBA.

Is PULSE available worldwide?

Availability may depend on jurisdiction and how underlying RWAs are structured. Always check local rules and project announcements. This website is not legal or financial advice.

What happens if something goes wrong?

Mature protocols define incident response: communication channels, mitigation steps, and post-mortems. As PULSE evolves, it should publish clear procedures for security incidents and operational issues.
